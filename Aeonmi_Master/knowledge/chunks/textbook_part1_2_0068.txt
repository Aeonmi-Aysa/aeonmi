=== What Is Aer? ===

BODY:
Aer (full name: Qiskit Aer) is IBM's high-performance quantum circuit simulator. It runs on your local machine using classical computation to simulate what a quantum computer would do. Aer is the default backend for quantum_run() in Aeonmi.

Unlike real quantum hardware, the Aer simulator:
- Has no queue time — results are instant
- Has no noise by default (though noise models can be added)
- Can simulate arbitrarily large circuits within memory limits
- Is completely free to use
- Requires no IBM account or token

For learning Aeonmi's quantum layer, Aer is everything you need. Real quantum hardware (IBM Brisbane) adds noise, queue times, and hardware-specific constraints. Aer is ideal for verifying that your circuit logic is correct before submitting to hardware.
